Pricing & Profitability Analysis – Retail Dataset

Problem:

The business lacked visibility on how discount strategies impact profitability and sales performance.

What I did:

  • Analysed relationships between sales, profit, and discounts
  • Identified unprofitable products and categories
  • Compared performance across categories and time
  • Built visualisations to explore pricing patterns
  • Developed an interactive dashboard

Key Insights:

  • High discounts often reduce profit margins
  • Furniture category shows frequent negative profit
  • Sales do not always increase with higher discounts
  • Discount strategies are often inefficient or misaligned

Tools:

Python, pandas, scikit-learn, Power BI

Result:

  • Identified profit leakage caused by discounting
  • Provided recommendations to optimise pricing strategy
  • Enabled better decision-making on discounts and margins

Crime Risk Analysis & Prediction – Ipswich (UK)

Problem:

Crime data from multiple years was inconsistent and difficult to analyse, limiting the ability to identify trends, high-risk areas, and support decision-making for local authorities.

What I did:

  • Cleaned and merged multi-year crime datasets (2022–2024)
  • Removed missing values, duplicates, and geographic outliers
  • Applied reverse geocoding to improve location accuracy
  • Engineered time-based features (year, month, seasonality)
  • Enriched data with socio-economic indicators (employment, activity rates)
  • Performed EDA to identify temporal and spatial patterns
  • Applied clustering (K-Means, DBSCAN) to detect crime zones
  • Built a Random Forest model to predict crime types
  • Developed an interactive dashboard using Streamlit

Key Insights:

  • Crime peaks consistently in July and December (seasonality)
  • Violence & sexual offences are the most frequent crimes
  • Specific streets (e.g. Dogs Head Street, Lloyds Avenue) are high-risk hotspots
  • Many crimes result in “no suspect identified” outcomes
  • Socio-economic variables showed weak correlation with crime levels
  • Clustering revealed distinct crime zones (e.g. low crime vs shoplifting areas)

Tools:

Python, pandas, scikit-learn, Plotly, Folium, Streamlit, Power Query

Result:

  • Identified crime hotspots and seasonal patterns
  • Built a predictive model (46–48% accuracy, F1 = 0.39)
  • Delivered an interactive dashboard for exploring trends and predictions
  • Enabled data-driven insights for local crime prevention strategies
